About Objective-C

Objective-C is a general-purpose, high-level, object-oriented programming language. It was first released in 1984 and was designed by Brad Cox.

In the Language Lineage dataset, its compiler is written in C++.

Objective-C drew on ideas from C and Smalltalk and went on to influence Swift and C++.
